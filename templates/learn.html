📚 Learn the Basics

Investing doesn't have to be complicated. Here are the foundational concepts every new investor should understand before putting money in the market.

🌱

Compound Interest

Your money earns interest. Then that interest earns interest. Over decades, this snowball effect can turn modest savings into significant wealth. Albert Einstein reportedly called it the "eighth wonder of the world."

🥚

Diversification

Don't put all your eggs in one basket. By spreading investments across different stocks, sectors, and asset types, you reduce the risk that one bad performer will wreck your portfolio.

⚖️

Risk vs. Reward

Higher potential returns come with higher risk. Stocks can soar but also crash. Bonds are safer but grow slowly. Your ideal mix depends on your timeline and how much volatility you can stomach.

📅

Dollar-Cost Averaging

Instead of trying to time the market (which even pros fail at), invest a fixed amount on a regular schedule. You'll buy more shares when prices are low and fewer when they're high, smoothing out volatility.

⏰

Time in the Market

"Time in the market beats timing the market." Starting at 22 vs. 32 with the same monthly contribution can mean hundreds of thousands more by retirement, thanks to compound interest doing its thing.

💼

Index Funds

Instead of picking individual stocks, you can buy a fund that holds the entire market (like the S&P 500). Low fees, instant diversification, and historically strong returns make this the go-to choice for most investors.
